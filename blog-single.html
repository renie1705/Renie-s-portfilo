The Women's Crime Analysis project focuses on exploring and understanding crime trends affecting women. Using crime-related datasets, the project analyzes various factors such as location, type of crime, and time trends. Women's crime in India is a critical issue that reflects deep-rooted societal challenges. According to reports, "crimes against women have been on the rise," with cases ranging from domestic violence to sexual harassment and human trafficking. Despite legal frameworks, "many women still face significant barriers in accessing justice." Experts argue that "cultural norms and social stigmas" often prevent victims from reporting crimes, further exacerbating the issue. The need for better enforcement, awareness, and support systems is clear, as "empowering women and ensuring their safety is crucial for societal progress."
